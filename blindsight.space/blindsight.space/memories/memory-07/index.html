Book Reference ↓
Chapter 2 / Peter Watts “Blindsight”

→It wasn't so much the way they looked. The elongate limbs, the pale skin, the canines and the extended mandible—noticeable, yes, even alien, but not disturbing, not frightening. Not even the eyes, really. The eyes of dogs and cats shine in the darkness; we don't shiver at the sight.

Not the way they looked. The way they moved.

Message: G-01
19/03/2017

↪︎The novel doesn’t have that many characters, which gave Peter Watts ample opportunity to describe each one in depth. We began our work on this part by finding suitable references for each of them. Apart from their appearance, we also needed to figure out their uniforms, find the right mix of recognizable, functional and futuristic.

Message: G-03
01/06/2018

↪︎We began modeling Sarasti off the concept-art, but then found an AMA of Peter Watts’ on Reddit, in which he mentioned Mads Mikkelsen as the perfect actor for Sarasti’s part. This made our job a lot easier - we took Mads’ appearance as our starting-off point and then tweaked it to fit the vampiric traits described in the book.

Next we needed to figure out skin and hair shading and create his outfit.
